About This Account

Stories Japan
Never Told

語られなかった物語

Eternal Sakura is a tribute account dedicated to preserving the untold stories of Japan's history — particularly the young men of the Special Attack Units (神風特攻隊), who gave their lives believing in something greater than themselves.

They were not just soldiers. They were sons, brothers, students — young men with dreams, with people they loved. Their final letters speak of cherry blossoms, of mothers, of a Japan they believed was worth dying for.

This account exists so that their voices are not silenced by time. Through photos, videos, and their own words — we share their humanity with the world.

このアカウントは、日本の歴史の中で語られることの少なかった物語を伝えるために作りました。
特攻隊員たちは、ただの兵士ではありませんでした。
彼らは夢を持ち、家族を愛した、普通の若者たちでした。
その記憶を、世界に届けたい。
Our Mission

Why We Remember

なぜ、伝えるのか

Remember

記憶する

Over 3,800 young men died as kamikaze pilots between 1944 and 1945. Most were between 17 and 24 years old. Their names deserve to be remembered — not as weapons, but as human beings.

Share

伝える

Their final letters are filled with love — for their mothers, for cherry blossoms, for the land they called home. We translate and share these words so the world can truly hear them.

Connect

繋がる

Beyond borders and beyond history, grief and love are universal. We believe that when the world sees their humanity, it changes how we understand war, sacrifice, and what truly matters.

"Mother, please stay well.
The cherry blossoms were so beautiful.
I love Japan. That is all."

「お母さん、どうか元気でいてください。
桜がとても綺麗でした。日本が好きです。ただそれだけです。」

— Final letter of a Kamikaze pilot, 1945
